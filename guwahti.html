Guwahati

Introduction Guwahati is the biggest city of the Indian state of Assam and also the largest metropolis in northeastern India. Dispur, the capital of Assam, is in the circuit city region located within Guwahati. The city is known as the "gateway to North East India".

Sualkuchi is a weaver’s town located on the north bank of Brahmaputra and is known as Manchester of East for its silk production. But if you think this is some cottage industrial town near Guwahati, let the vivid images of silk mekhela prove you wrong. What to do: Shop Mekhela Chadar and traditional bridal dresses as a souvenir from your trip.
A place for art lovers and intellectuals, Kalakshetra is an extraordinary platform for artists to showcase their talents. You can enjoy drama, dance, and music performances in the open air theatre and admire the display of beautiful paintings and artefacts in the museum. There is even a library and children’s park for your leisure. If you like all things art, this one of the things to do in Guwahati, Assam. Timing: 10.00 am to 7.00 pm Tip: Do try cable car ride facility available inside the park to commute the tourists
Assam State Museum – Soak In The Culture Curious about the local Ahom culture and folklore? Head straight to Assam State Museum which will give you a real taste of the destination. It is known as one of the best museums in North India which houses artefacts as old as 7th century. History buffs cannot find better things to do in Guwahati, India than this. Guwahati Planetarium – Star-gazing When was the last time you looked at a sky full of stars? If you can’t remember, it is a reason enough for you to be enthralled with a heavenly experience. Guwahati Planetarium is a place for astro-lovers which projects great sky-watching sessions. The shows are available at regular intervals and available in English, Hindi, and Assamese languages. Timings: 11:00 AM to 4:00 PM Entry Fee: INR 30
Umananda Temple is located on Peacock Island amidst the Brahmaputra River. The 17th century temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is believed to be the place where he burnt Kamadev to ashes during meditation. It is one of the most revered temples in Guwahati. The recreational boat ride is one of the things to do near Guwahati. You will also meet notorious Golden Langoors on your way. It takes half an hour to reach temple by motorable boat. Timing: 5.30 am to 6 pm Boat ride: INR 50 per head
Assam State Zoo – An Amazing Place For Family Outing assam state zoo If you are visiting Guwahati with family, this could be a great attraction for the kids. The zoo is pretty large and houses a number of animals like rhinos, monkeys, and pelicans. It is vibrant with exotic flora and fauna and is located in the beautiful setting of Madgharia hills. One of the best tourist places in Asaam, this zoo has expanded over the years and has over 900 species of birds, animals and reptiles. Timing: 8 am to 4.30 pm Entry Fee: INR 30 per person Tip: If you want to observe the animal closely safari is the best option
Deepor Beel – Dilly Dally If you are someone who likes to spend time by the waterbody introspecting, there is no better place than Deepor Beel. It is a freshwater lake lying towards the south-west of Guwahati. Considered as one of the biggest lakes in Brahmaputra Valley, it is a paradise for bird-watchers. Winters are the best time to spot migratory birds which fly down from the mountains to more pleasant weather in plains.
